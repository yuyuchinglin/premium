Phase I

User Research

Product Background

What is Synology Calendar Missing for Enterprise?

Synology Calendar was in maintenance mode for a long time. Now, as we focus on enterprise solutions, we aim to understand user habits and close the gaps in adoption, ensuring stronger support for enterprise communication.

Synology Calendar in 2023
·   ·   ·

Research Method

Questionnaires & Interviews

Since our company allows voluntary adoption of Synology Calendar, we conducted a survey of 140 participants (20% of our company). Among them, only 58.6% use it, with a satisfaction rating of 3.3/5. We then interviewed six participants for further insights.

Questionnaire Collection in Our Company's Chat System
·   ·   ·

Research Insight #1

Users Struggle with CalDAV Sync for Mobile Access

Among other Calendar users, 96.3% rely on mobile devices, highlighting mobility's importance. However, Synology Calendar lacks an app, and only 37.8% sync it to another mobile client via CalDAV.

During interviews, some users mentioned they were unaware of CalDAV, while others reported encountering numerous issues when attempting to use it.

Pain points when syncing Synology Calendar to other clients via CalDAV
·   ·   ·

Research Insight #2

Synology Calendar Has UI/UX Issues and Missing Key Features

Many users reported UI/UX issues with the web version, including limited fields for quick event creation and unintuitive feature placement. Additionally, the lack of essential features like system integration and meeting room booking negatively impacts user engagement and experience.

Synology Calendar has UI/UX issues
·   ·   ·

Feature Roadmap

Since mobility is key for Calendar usage, and CalDAV is outside our control, in Q1 2023, the PM proposed a roadmap to the CEO:

  • 2024 Q2 - Develop Calendar Mobile App
  • 2025 Q1 - Improve Calendar UI/UX Issues
  • 2026 Q1 - Develop Meeting Room Booking Feature

Phase II

Calendar Mobile App

To boost MailPlus license sales and enhance service for Synology enterprise users, the PM decided to integrate Calendar into MailPlus Mobile first, with plans to support an independent Calendar app in the future. This strategy aimed to make MailPlus more valuable for enterprise customers by bundling calendar functionality into the existing product.

Research Method

Follow-up Interviews for Mobile Needs

The Calendar mobile app is a large-scale feature, and it was unclear what to prioritize. To define the mobile-specific needs, we selected 6 participants from our initial survey and conducted follow-up interviews focused on how they use calendars on mobile devices.

·   ·   ·

Design Challenge

How might we create a calendar app in MailPlus Mobile that improves mobile productivity and strengthens user engagement?

·   ·   ·

Research Insight #1

Users Need Both Broad and Detailed Calendar Views

There are various calendar views, such as agenda, day, week, and month. My research found these views fall into two usage categories:

  • Broad View (Week/Month): Provides an overview for tasks like checking availability, holidays, project timelines, and schedules. The month view was more popular.
  • Detailed View (Agenda/Day): Focuses on the current day, emphasizing time and location, and is typically suited for mobile displays.

Each view has its pros and cons, with users selecting them based on their context and habits.

Although both are called Month view on iOS Calendar, the one on the left provides an overview, serving as a broad view, while the one on the right focuses only on today, acting as a detailed view.
·   ·   ·

Research Insight #2

Users Want Personal Customization in Shared Calendars

Most enterprise users rely on Synology's Shared Calendar as a team bulletin board but want personal customization options, as all event settings are currently shared. The core problem: users are overwhelmed by too many irrelevant events from shared calendars, making it hard to focus on what matters to them.

Design Assumptions

Build a Work-Friendly Calendar in MailPlus Mobile

Based on our research and tight timeline, we focused on two key design assumptions to validate:

01  

Broad and detailed calendar views enhance event visibility and planning efficiency on mobile

02  

Personalized customization in Synology Calendar and deep MailPlus integration can increase user engagement

·   ·   ·

UX Design

Making Work Click: Calendar for MailPlus Mobile

I designed the detailed interaction flows for the Calendar in MailPlus Mobile, covering calendar views, event details, editing features, notifications, and settings—while collaborating closely with the UI designer and technical writer. This portfolio outlines only the main user flow.

·   ·   ·

UX Highlight #1

Diverse Calendar View

  • Agenda View: Provides accessibility with larger text and a clearer layout
  • Day View: Focuses on time and location, ideal for mobile and daily planning
  • Month View: Offers a quick overview, perfect for planning and scheduling

To ensure consistency, clicking a date in any view (Month, Week, or Agenda) navigates to the Day View, the central unit for all views. (rationale)

Diverse Calendar View
·   ·   ·

UX Highlight #2

Personalized Customization: "Hide Event" Feature

Research Insight #2 revealed that users felt overwhelmed by irrelevant events in shared calendars. We explored several options — including personal notes, personal tags, and custom color coding — to give users more control.

Ultimately, we chose the "hide event" feature because it directly addresses the core problem: too many irrelevant events cluttering the calendar. While notes and tags add information, hiding events reduces noise — allowing users to remove unrelated events and keep their calendar focused on what matters.

Personalized Customization: "Hide Event" Feature
·   ·   ·

UX Highlight #3

Work Seamlessly with MailPlus

Quickly turn emails into calendar events and switch between MailPlus and Calendar with a single tap — a seamless productivity feature unique to Synology, not available in Gmail or Outlook.

Work Seamlessly with MailPlus

Result

Validating Our Design Assumptions

After launching the Calendar in MailPlus Mobile, we were able to validate both design assumptions:

Assumption 01 — Confirmed: Users actively engage with multiple calendar views, with 82% of users using the Month View. The diverse views enhance event visibility and planning efficiency, as reflected in positive user feedback on the calendar experience.

Assumption 02 — Confirmed: The personalized customization and MailPlus integration increased user engagement, evidenced by a 42% increase in license sales and a 20% boost in MAU.

Lesson Learned

The Power of Storytelling in UX Research, and the Importance of Clear Communication

This project taught me two things I'll carry forward.

First, I learned that UX research can create massive organizational impact when the story is told compellingly. The research itself was rigorous, but what truly moved the CEO to commit US$600K was how the findings were presented — connecting user pain points to business opportunities in a way that resonated deeply. It's not enough to have good data; you need to make people feel why it matters.

Second, working closely with the mobile RD team reinforced the importance of clear and honest communication. Our different roles required frequent alignment, and I learned that the more transparent and direct the communication, the smoother the collaboration. What I cherish most is the trust and understanding we built through this process — it made the project not just successful, but truly rewarding.
