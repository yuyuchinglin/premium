Tea Shop’s Application

A tea shop application which allows for online ordering, lottery games, and marketing events.

·   ·   ·

Overview

Wang Lao Ji (WLJ) is a very popular tea brand in China; however, the brand and flavor is new to the Taiwanese people. Our proprietor introduces this brand to Taiwan and uses WLJ tea products as a base in creating new beverages, which form the foundation of a brand new tea shop, 1828 WLJ Tea Shop. There are seven branches in Taiwan. This brand is creative and enticing, since the beverage’s name and design are inspired by traditional Chinese stories.

Company: 1828 WLJ Tea Shop (a famous brand in China)

Role: UX/UI Design, Front-end Development

Time: 2018/10–2019/01 (full-time)

Our brand was created by an experienced graphic designer, and it is intended to be creative and enticing.
·   ·   ·

Business Goal - Challenge & Solution

Challenge
According to the data from our POS system, we have discovered that 80% of our customers are first time customers, and 20% are returning customers. This application’s business goal is to convert new customers to repeat customers. The two challenges we address are
1. To convert first-time customers to repeat customers
2. To make returning guest to buy the drink more often

Solution
To appeal to new customers, we offer coupons that allow them to try our beverages at a lower cost. Additionally, we utilize some long-term games, such as a lottery game and location check-ins, which requires time to receive rewards and encourages long-term engagement.

Competitor Analysis - DaYung's Tea Shop's Application

Advangtages
Since this tea shop is very popular and has several branches in Taiwan, they have many loyal customers. The main function of their application is for their customers to collect points or view information about the tea shop. In conclusion, there are two main advantages:
1. The application offers a loyalty program that rewards frequent visitors.
2. The information is clear and concise.

Disadvangtages
This application appears to be only for long-term or repeat customers. However, since their loyalty program offers a physical card, it’s not necessary for customers to collect points on the application. They also do not use the application to market any special events, which could appeal to new or potential customers. To sum it up, there are two disadvantages:
1. It’s not necessary for their loyalty program.
2. They do not market for special events that could appeal to new or potential customers.

Lottery Game Interface

When customers buy our drinks or visit our application, they receive one point to use towards the lottery game, which offers prizes to customers. People who use multiple points in one lottery game will have a better chance of winning the prize. For example, an iPhone requires 30,000 points. If a customer uses one point towards this prize, then he will have a 1/30,000 chance to win this prize. Because we offer many great prizes, customers will use a few points toward different prizes, which means that the prizes will take longer to win.

I have suggested an idea that we put a ranking on the number of points our customers use towards their prizes. For example, when a person plays a basketball shooting machine, a high score record is displayed, which the person always wants to beat. Many other individuals are driven by a desire to win. If we have a top ten ranking of how many points users dedicated towards each prize, it could encourage customers to use more points.

Special Event - Shake Your Phone

Our customers seemed to only open the application when they wanted to buy beverages. To increase engagement, we created an event wherein customers could obtain a discount coupon once per day by opening our application and shaking their phones. When customers open our application and shake their phones during the event period, we will give them a coupon for 60%, 50%, 30%, or 20% off according to a certain probability. It is a persuasive strategy in interaction design intended to encourage long-term engagement and customer loyalty.

It is also an aesthetically pleasing design that is inspired by traditional Chinese culture. An Imperial decree which contains a coupon will expand when customers shake their phones. This event is available by web view on our iOS and Android application, so I designed and hand-coded it.

Customer Experience in Tea Shop Store

Problem
When there are some events being marketed in our application, I often see many customers in our store downloading and using the application. Some customers may feel the application poses a security risk or that it is inconvenient to download on their phone. We wanted to make customers felt comfortable downloading the application instead of feeling pressured.

Many people are busy downloading our application to get coupons while they are in line.

Solution
We should not force new customers to download our application when they visit the store. Instead, we should offer coupons and games in stores so that all of our customers can access our promotions equally.

To encourage customers to use our application, we can put a sticker on their cups. The stickers could contain a serial number or QR code that directs customers to our application, where they can begin collecting points or learning about our special events.

Put a sticker containing a serial number on cups to encourage customers to download our application.

Project Learnings

Communication is important.
I am aware of the large gap between users and developers. For example, some functions are very helpful and user-friendly, thereby engaging users. However, developers may need to spend a lot of time on these. Other times, there are technical restrictions that delay the planning or completion of a project. Thus, to achieve a near-perfect product, communication is very important. Moreover, I am impressed by our project manager because he excelled at communicating, always focused on the common goal rather than any individual, and he did not show any emotions while communicating. He always worked smoothly, gaining everybody’s respect. I believe that I must learn and develop these qualities.
