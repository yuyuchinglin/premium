Tea Shop’s Application

A tea shop application which allows for online ordering, lottery games, and marketing events based on insights and pain points.

·   ·   ·

Overview

1828 WLJ Tea Shop has seven branches in Taiwan and is creative and enticing since the beverage’s name and design are all inspired by traditional Chinese stories. 1828 WLJ Tea Shop uses Chinese Wang Lao Ji (WLJ) tea products in creating new beverages. Wang Lao Ji is a very popular tea brand in China; however, the brand and flavor are new to the Taiwanese people.

The Challenge

1828 WLJ Tea Shop are expanding and growing from one branch to seven branches. Our shop has spent a lot of money on doing the marketing events and promotion. So, we got a lot of first-time customers. However, it is not easy for us to retain first-time customers and appeal customers without promotion. So, there are two main business goals that we address about:
(1) Converting existing customers to purchase drinks more frequently
(2) Applealing more potential customers

Insights & Pain Points

According to the problem, persona, user journey map, and survey. We organized and concluded that there are three insights and pain points.

1. People care about the price.
  • 70% of Taiwanese people want to get a discounted price for their drinks in a tea shop application. So, it will be applealing to them if a tea shop application contains coupons, lastest marketing events, and loyal card.
  • Our drink is too expensive. The beverage price in our tea shop is between NT$60 and NT$80; however, the general beverage price at other tea shops ranges between NT$30 and NT$60. Customers don't understand why we are more expensive than other tea shops.
2. People care about their time.
  • In Taiwan, people always have to wait in line when they want to purchase a drink, so 45.3% of people think that it is nice to have an online ordering mechanism in the application, even far beyond the delivery service (15.3%).
  • Downloading the application before checking out to get coupons is very troublesome and inconvenient. Some customers may feel the application poses a security risk to download and log into on their phones. Also, if they are busy, it might be an obstacle to them purchasing a drink.
People are busy downloading our application to get coupons while lining up.
3. People care about flavor.
  • When Taiwanese people choose their favorite tea shop, 91.1% of Taiwanese love a tea shop is because of its flavor, which far beyond other factors like price(27.2%) or brand(17.8%).
  • Some first-time customers think the flavor of our drinks is not acceptable, since our signature drinks are mixed with traditional Chinese medicine. Taiwanese people prefer sweet milk tea with boba or pure green tea instead.

Part I

Lower the threshold for customers to buy beverages

Solving Pain Points I: Price Issue

From our insight, we acknowlege that people regard discounts as the most important thing in the tea shop application. So, we designed four mechanism that people can get lower prices or rewards from the application.

Membership

Four membership levels to encourage existing tea shop fans to buy more.

Coupons

Customers can receive coupons through marketing events or activities.

Loyal Card

Customers can collect points in the loyal card which can get rewards in the future.

Pick-up Later

Customers can buy beverages during the marketing events, and pick them up later.

Membership, Coupons, Loyal Card, & Pick-up Later

Solving Pain Points II: Time Issue

(1) Use Stickers to Encourage Downloading Application After Buying

New customers should not be forced to download our application when they visit our store. Instead, we should place a sticker on their cups that advertises and encourages customers to download our application. The stickers could contain a serial number or QR code that directs customers to our application, which they can use to begin collecting points or learning about our special events.

Put a sticker containing a serial number on cups.
(2) Order Beverages through the Application

Ordering beverages is an important part of our application, which can actually be used to generate revenue. However, it's very complex, since there are many different orders and payment methods that can be used. The process of creating this interface required many discussions and testing phases.

User interface of beverage ordering process.

Solving Pain Points III: Flavor Issue

Localize the Flavor

Our singnature drink is Wang Lao-Ji, a popular herbal tea in China; however, some Taiwanese do not accept the flavor. Our beverage department has tried to launch more new drinks based on the flavor which Taiwanese familiar with. In the future, we also planned to have a beverage evaluation in our application.

Part II

Enhance customers' motivation to buy beverages and use the application

The Challenge

According to the data from our application, over 80% of our customers seemed to only open the application when they wanted to buy beverages.

How might we enhance customers' motivation to buy beverages and use the application?

·   ·   ·

Ideation for Enhancing Customers' Motivation

To increase engagement, our marketing team want to send coupons or valuable prizes to appeal to customers through different events.

Low-fidelity user flow of lottery game.
·   ·   ·

Regular Event: Lottery Game

When customers buy our drinks or visit our application, they receive one ticket to use towards the lottery game which offers big prizes. People who use more tickets in one lottery game will have a better chance of winning the prize. I suggested an idea that putting a top ten ranking on the number of tickets customers used below the prizes. Many individuals will driven by a desire to win and use more points.

Low-fidelity user flow of lottery game.
Lottery game's high-fidelity prototype.
·   ·   ·

Special Event: Shake Your Phone

To increase engagement, we also created an irregular marketing event when customers open our application and shake their phones, they will receive a coupon for a random discount once per day. This event is available by web view on our iOS and Android application, so I designed and hand-coded it.
