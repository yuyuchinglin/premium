Tea Shop’s Application

An application that designed to lower the threshold for customers to buy beverages and increase customers' engagement based on users' pain points.

·   ·   ·
Overview

1828 WLJ Tea Shop uses a Chinese popular herbal tea, Wang Lao Ji (WLJ), in creating new beverages. The brand is creative and has seven branches in Taiwan.

I was the sole UX designer in the software team who is responsible for user research, UX/UI design, and some front-end development. We had a graphic design team who is responsible for branding, etc.

Part I

Lower the threshold for customers to buy beverages

Business Goal

1828 WLJ Tea Shop is a new brand and is growing from one branch to seven branches in Taiwan. Our shop has spent a lot of money on doing the marketing events and promotion. However, it is not easy for us to retain existing customers and appeal new customers without promotion.

Insights & Pain Points

According to the problem, persona, user journey map, and survey. We organized and concluded that there are three insights and pain points.

1. People care about the price.
  • 70% of Taiwanese people want to get a discounted price for their drinks in a tea shop application. So, it will be applealing to them if a tea shop application contains coupons, lastest marketing events, and loyal card.
  • Our drink is too expensive. The beverage price in our tea shop is between NT$60 and NT$80; however, the general beverage price at other tea shops ranges between NT$30 and NT$60. Customers don't understand why we are more expensive than other tea shops.
How might we use the application to provide customers a better price?
2. People care about their time.
  • In Taiwan, people always have to wait in line when they want to purchase a drink, so 45.3% of people think that it is nice to have an online ordering mechanism in the application, even far beyond the delivery service (15.3%).
  • Downloading the application before checking out to get coupons is very troublesome and inconvenient. Some customers may feel the application poses a security risk to download and log into on their phones. Also, if they are busy, it might be an obstacle to them purchasing a drink.
People are busy downloading our application to get coupons while lining up.
How might we shorter the time customers spend on buying beverages?
3. People care about flavor.
  • When Taiwanese people choose their favorite tea shop, 91.1% of Taiwanese love a tea shop is because of its flavor, which far beyond other factors like price(27.2%) or brand(17.8%).
  • Some first-time customers think the flavor of our drinks is not acceptable, since our signature drinks are mixed with traditional Chinese medicine. Taiwanese people prefer sweet milk tea with boba or pure green tea instead.
How might we provide a widely accepted taste?

Redesign the User Flows

I proposed the new user flows and learned that it is important to make customers love the beverage's flavor and download the application to be our member. These two factors form a positive cycle. Moreover, when there is a long queue, ordering online is a definitely must-to-have feature.

·   ·   ·

Solving Pain Points I: Flavor Issue

(1) Localize the flavor

Our singnature beverage is Wang Lao-Ji, a popular herbal tea in China; however, some Taiwanese do not accept the flavor. Therefore, our beverage department has tried to launch new flavors that suit Taiwanese people.

Localize the flavor
(2) Let customers evaluate our new flavors

Provide free tasting and invite customers to vote! Since we want the flavor is appealing to potential customers not our loyal customers, it is better to put a board in our shop and hold a tasting event.

Concept board of "Vote for the new flavor!"
·   ·   ·

Solving Pain Points II: Price Issue

From our insight, we acknowlege that people regard discounts as the most important thing in the tea shop application. So, we designed four mechanisms that people can get lower prices or rewards from the application.

Membership

Four membership levels to encourage existing tea shop fans to buy more.

Coupons

Customers can receive coupons through marketing events or activities.

Loyal Card

Customers can collect points in the loyal card which can get rewards in the future.

Membership, coupons, and loyal card (cooperate with other graphic designers)
·   ·   ·

Solving Pain Points III: Time Issue

(1) Use stickers to encourage customers downloading application

New customers should not be forced to download our application when they just visit our store. Instead, we should place a sticker containing a serial number or QR code that encourages customers to download our application, which they can use to begin collecting points or getting coupons.

Put a sticker containing a serial number on cups.
(2) Order beverages through the application

To shorter buying time, customers can order beverages and pay online through our app. They will receive a number to help them pickup. However, it's very complex, since there are many different orders and payment methods that can be used. The process of creating this interface required many discussions and testing phases.

User interface of beverage ordering process.

Part II

Increase customers' engagement with the application

The Challenge

According to the data from our application, we have more than 7,500 members; however, over 80% of them only open the application when they wanted to buy beverages.

How might we enhance customers' motivation to buy beverages and use the application?

·   ·   ·

Insights & Pain Points

Look deeper into customers' motivation in our application, we discovered some insights and pain points.

1. Discounts or rewards will definitely drive individuals' motivation

Over 70% of Taiwanese people want to get a discounted price for their drinks in a tea shop application. They regard discounts as the most important and attractive thing in the application.

2. People seldom share our application on their social media

People like to share some cool experiences or beautiful photos on their social media. Not only they satisfies their motivation, but also a promotion for business. However, people often share photos with our beverages in our tea shop but seldom share our application.

People often share photos in our tea shop but seldom share our application.
3. Our application lack of updates

People often open an application frequently is because of the lastest news or social updates; however, in our application, since we only have two or three marketing events a month, we seldom update. So, people do not often open our application. Maybe a daily checkout or a long-term game are recommended.

We do not have lots of marketing events to update.

Regular Event: Lottery Game

When customers buy our beverages or visit our application, they receive one ticket to use towards the lottery game which offers big prizes. Customers who use more tickets in one lottery game will have a better chance of winning the prize. I suggested a idea that putting a top ten ranking on the number of tickets customers used below the prizes. Many individuals will driven by a desire to win and use more tickets.

Special Event: Shake Your Phone

To increase engagement, we also created an irregular marketing event when customers open our application and shake their phones, they will receive a coupon for a random discount once per day. This event is available by web view on our iOS and Android application, so I designed and hand-coded it.
