MailPlus Mobile AI Integration

Designed AI email composition and summarization flows for mobile—making powerful AI feel intuitive and familiar.

·   ·   ·
Overview

Generative AI has become a buzzword in recent years, shaping the future of technology. Companies are exploring AI with new interaction designs, but integrating it into applications in a user-friendly way is still challenging.

I will explore how to use research findings and design principles to integrate AI into MailPlus mobile as a UX designer. I think it's an inspiring interaction design case :)

Project Background

Integrating AI into MailPlus Mobile

The core functions of email are writing and reading, and Generative AI enhances both workflows:

  • Email Writing: AI Assistant generates and refines email content
  • Email Reading: AI Summary provides concise email summaries

Part I

AI Assistant Helps Compose Emails

Research Findings #1

Users are Accustomed to AI Chatbots

Users already familiar with generative AI chatbots such as ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot will seek assistance from these platforms when facing challenges. These platforms represent the primary competition for AI interaction within the application.

AI Chatbots
·   ·   ·

Research Findings #2

People Prefer Tapping Options Rather Than Typing on Mobile Devices

MailPlus web AI design focuses on conversational interactions. In mobile scenarios, users generally prefer to quickly tap options to reach their goals, except in specific situations.

Focus on tapping over typing on mobile devices
·   ·   ·

Research Finding #3

MailPlus' Toolbar is Positioned Beyond Comfortable Reach

Users generally navigate mobile devices with a single thumb. However, the MailPlus mobile app currently places the toolbar in the top-right corner. This positioning can be challenging for users, especially right-handed ones.

MailPlus composer overlaid with the right-hand reach comfort zone.
·   ·   ·

Design Goals

Engaging AI Seamlessly in the Application Flow

Users integrate our application into their daily routines, which is our greatest strength. Increased AI interaction with the app’s flow makes our AI interactions essential.

With that in mind, here are three design principles:

01  Visible Access

02 Intuitive

UX Design

AI Assistant in the Composer's User Flow

AI Assistant can help users polish text and generate ideas. The detailed user flow is quite complex, so I’m only demonstrating the main scenario—polishing text—in this portfolio. The blue background labels highlight key UX design elements.

·   ·   ·

UX Highlight #1

Reachable Entry Point

Research Finding #3 reveals that the toolbar in the top-right corner is difficult to reach. To improve accessibility, we have moved it above the keyboard, where it appears only when users focus on the email content. Furthermore, our AI entry point is designed to be more accessible than Gmail's, offering a smoother user experience.

AI Entry Point
·   ·   ·

UX Highlight #2

Select AI Options Effortlessly

(1) Specify the Refined Scope

Our AI feature targets the entire text or a selected range, so I highlight the refined text to show the user the scope.

(2) Focus on Tapping Options

Research finding #2 shows that tapping is more important than typing in the prompt bar. Therefore, I have placed the tapping options on the first page and set the prompt bar to navigate to the next page.

Select AI Options Effortlessly
·   ·   ·

UX Highlight #3

Read AI Draft Intuitively

(1) AI Draft Directly Replaces Content

Our AI-generated content directly replaces the original text to help users understand the effect of replacing text.

(2) Pull Up Offers More Refined Options

The bottom sheet shows only the essential “Keep” and “Discard” buttons by default, providing users ample space to read the AI draft comfortably. Furthermore, we have left some space to subtly indicate that users can pull up the button sheet for additional refined options.

Read AI Draft Intuitively

Part II

AI Summary Aids in Reading Emails

Research Findings #1

People Use the AI Summary Only in Certain Situations

Although MailPlus is designed for work, most users find the subject line to be sufficient for understanding most emails. When they read the body, it's usually effortless. AI Summary is only used for lengthy emails or extended threads.

I mostly don't read emails except for HR announcements, which are easy to read and don't need AI summary.

— A Research Developer

·   ·   ·

Research Findings #2

Users Require Varying Summary Lengths Based on Context

Users often favor a more detailed summary when preparing to take the next action, such as replying. Conversely, when they want to quickly review the content, they favor a brief summary.

If I'm not the intended person, a one-sentence brief is sufficient. However, if I need to take actions, I feel this version of the summary lacks essential information.

— A Survey from Proof of Concept (POC)

·   ·   ·

UX Design

AI Summary's User Flow

AI Summary helps users reading lengthy emails or extended email threads. The blue background labels highlight key UX design elements.

AI Summary's user flow
·   ·   ·

UX Highlight #1

Clear Entry Flow

(1) Clear, Simple, and Direct Entry Point

Text is easier to understand than an AI icon, as the AI summary is difficult to convey through an icon.

(2) Select Summary Type Based on Context

Based on research findings #1 & #2, people don’t use the AI summary frequently and choose its length based on context. Therefore, we believe adding an extra step is acceptable.

Clear Entry Flow
·   ·   ·

UX Highlight #2

Bold Key Points for Easier Reading

Since plain text can be difficult to read, especially with long summaries, we ask the AI to highlight important information such as names and dates in bold for better readability.

Bold Key Points for Easier Reading

Conclusion

Results

Superior UX Compared to Gmail's AI Feature

Since the feature hasn't been released yet, we lack data to support the design. However, compared to competing products, I still believe we've done an excellent job. 🤩

(1) Compared to Gmail

Billions of people worldwide use Gmail, which offers well-designed features overall. However, its AI features can be more intuitive and user-friendly.

Gmail's AI Assistant User Flow
(2) Compared to iOS Mail

iOS Mail's AI Assistant offers a comprehensive set of features, and its user flow is simpler and more intuitive than Gmail’s. However, the only drawback is that the “Original” and “Rewrite” buttons are difficult to interpret.

iOS Mail's AI Assistant User Flow

Project Learning

AI Brings New UX Design Challenges

AI is still developing, leading to non-standardized UX interactions where typical design solutions are not applicable. This has made it one of the most exciting design challenges I've faced recently. Throughout this challenge, I’ve learned to embrace change and focus on the fundamental use requirement to create better designs. 😊✨
