IoT Device Management

Users can remotely organize, update, and monitor their devices at scale.

·   ·   ·

Overview

Introduction

Our company produces and sells numerous tablet computers for military and industrial purposes. In the past, if we wanted to update the firmware or software on the tablets, we had to manually update them one-by-one using cables that plug into the computer. This method was inconvenient since the company has a lot of tablets. As a result, we created an IoT device management cloud service to manage and control our tablets through a cloud server.

Research Insight

Real-time monitoring, a remote control function, and a notification mechanism are must-haves.
Generally, the system integrators identified three wants: (1) real-time monitoring, (2) a remote control function, and (3) a notification mechanism.
It's critical for system integrators to receive notifications immediately when devices accidentally shut down.
The Internet of Vehicles (IoV)'s system integrator would prefer to receive notifications from any location.
Since there is a multitude of devices, it's also important that we can sync them at one time. For example, it would be useful to be able to turn off 100 devices’ WiFi modification simultaneously.
The layout is too simple. The gray is too light. SI customers are not used to the minimalist style.
Compared to functions, IoV customers usually don’t place as much emphasis on the interface. However, the primary color of the interface is red, which can be easily confused for an alert or warning message.

Goal & Hypotheses

The goal is to launch a customer version of IoT Device Management that would enhance their experiences with real-time monitoring, remote controlling, and notifications.

Hypothesis 1:
Publishing these user-friendly functions will create additional revenue from our system integrator customers.

Hypothesis 2:
Improving the consistency of the user interface will improve customer satisfaction, lessen the time spent on the interface, and reduce the misclick rate.

Constraints
Some of the constraints include a limited timeframe and a change in the UI layout without altering the basic structure.

Lesson Learned

1) Design and development is iterative

Iterations are very valuable when it comes to learning from past versions, seeing the possibility of the good that can come out of it. Although I did a lot of preparation for the design and proposed the best possible version, many problems still occurred during and/or after developing the whole website. I changed my prototype many times, and I believe that it is best to think about the big picture while redesigning it. Therefore, I learned that only iterative design can come close to being perfect.

2) Having ideas or opinions before meetings

Our company holds many meetings. I discovered that if people did not have ideas or opinions beforehand, they will be easily influenced by others’ opinions. This makes it difficult to reach a good conclusion during our discussions, since we do not have enough time or clear thoughts to draw such a conclusion. Therefore, I believe that having ideas or opinions before group discussions is best in order to promote deep and high-quality discussions and conclusions.

3) Soft Skills

In this project, I have learned that UX is for users as well as stakeholders. It is important for me to determine the critical needs of our stakeholders and then respond to them. Furthermore, there are many benefits to involving multiple constituents in the UX process. Engineers often prefer to focus on what needs modification and do not care to discuss alternative ideas. As a result, I summarized various ideas into a list that identified pertinent changes that needed to be made by engineers. However, on occasion, I will share some interesting UX insights with them so that we can grow together.
